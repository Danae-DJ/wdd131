Our Purpose

The purpose of the page is to help people, regardless of their age, choose a bicycle appropriate to the activities around them. Cycling: Ways to Practice

Cycling is a versatile sport and activity that involves riding a bicycle for various purposes, such as transportation, recreation, fitness, or competition. It's a fantastic way to improve physical health, explore the outdoors, and challenge oneself physically and mentally.

There are some ways to practice cycling:

    Recreational Cycling: Casual riding for fun, relaxation, and general fitness.

    Road Cycling: Riding on paved roads, often for long distances. Includes endurance cycling, group rides, and competitive road racing.

    Mountain Biking (MTB): Riding off-road on trails, tackling rugged terrain, steep climbs, and technical descents.

    Cyclocross: A mix of road cycling, mountain biking, and running, involving off-road courses with obstacles.

    Track Cycling: High-speed cycling on a velodrome, a banked oval track.

    Commuting: Using a bike as a primary mode of transportation in urban or suburban areas.

    BMX: Performing stunts, tricks, or racing on BMX bikes, often on dirt tracks or urban obstacles.

    Gravel Riding: Exploring unpaved roads or mixed surfaces, a blend of road and mountain biking.

    Touring: Long-distance travel by bike, often carrying supplies for multiple days of adventure.

Bicycles: which is the best for me?

Each cycling discipline requires a specific type of bike designed for the terrain and purpose:

    Road Bikes: Lightweight with narrow tires and drop handlebars. Best for paved roads and speed. Ideal for endurance riding, racing, or fitness.

    Mountain Bikes (MTB): Sturdy frame, wide tires, and suspension (front, rear, or both). Designed for off-road trails and rough terrain. Variants: Cross-country, trail, downhill, and enduro bikes.

    Hybrid Bikes: Blend of road and mountain bike features. Comfortable upright position, suitable for commuting and light off-road trails.

    Gravel Bikes: Drop handlebars like a road bike but wider tires for unpaved roads. Versatile for mixed surfaces and adventure riding.

    BMX Bikes: Compact and durable for stunts, tricks, and racing. Single-speed with small wheels and high maneuverability.

    Track Bikes: Fixed gear with no brakes. Designed for velodrome racing.

    Touring Bikes: Built for long distances with racks for carrying gear. Sturdy and comfortable for multi-day rides.

    Electric Bikes (e-Bikes): Equipped with a motor to assist pedaling. Suitable for commuting, recreation, or mountain biking.

    Fat Bikes: Oversized tires for sand, snow, or loose terrain. Great for extreme environments.

By identifying your goals and terrain, you can choose the right bike and gear to enjoy cycling to its fullest!
